 <p>Caffeine is a bitter, white crystalline purine, methyl xanthine alkaloid, which is founded in coffee, tea, soft drinks, chocolate, kola nuts, and certain medicines. Unlike many other psychoactive substances, it is legal and unregulated in nearly all parts of the world.Caffeine is a central nervous system (CNS) stimulant of the methyl xanthine class. It is one of the most popular drugs in the world, consumed by up to 90% of people in the world in one form or another, but mostly in beverages. It is a naturally occurring substance found in plants like cocoa beans, tea leaves, and kola nuts. For most people, the amount of caffeine in two to four cups of coffee a day is not harmful. However, too much caffeine can cause problems.</p>
