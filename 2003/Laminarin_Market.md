 <p>The molecule laminarin (also known as laminaran) is a storage glucan (a polysaccharide of glucose) found in brown algae. It is used as a carbohydrate food reserve in the same way that chrysolaminarin is used by phytoplankton. It is created by photosynthesis and is made up of (13)-glucan with (16)-linkages. It is a linear polysaccharide, with a (13):(16) ratio of 3:1.</p>
