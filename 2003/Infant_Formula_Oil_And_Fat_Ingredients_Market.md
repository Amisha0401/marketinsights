 <p>The human infant was designed to consume a high-fat diet. Roughly 50% of the calories in breast milk come from fat. All baby formulas provide roughly that much fat as well. Fat consists of smaller components known as fatty acids. Baby formulas all contain a blend of different fats and oils to provide the infant with a fatty acid composition similar to that of breast milk. The most common oils could be seen in baby formula ingredients are: palm olein oil, soy oil, coconut oil, high oleic sunflower oil and high oleic safflower oil, in various combinations.</p>
